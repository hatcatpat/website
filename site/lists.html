a catalog things, for remembering and variety

watched
  • the return of the pink panther [1975]: reasonably funny slapstick comedy, that maintains a rather loose connection to any sort of coherent plot. i also watched the first half of the original "pink panther", but stopped due to it being an utter slog, which contains unashamed date-rape. the only moments of enjoyment come from the inspector (peter sellers) himself, which the creators clearly realised as the later films all focus around him. in particular, he gains a ludicrously french accent in the later films. its the phonetic equivalent of wearing a beret, eating a croissant, drinking coffee and smoking a cig on a balcony overlooking the eiffel tower.
  • harvie krumpet [2003]: horrendously delightful short claymation focusing on the unfortunate life of a squishy downtrodden polish man, whose head is full of questionable fakts about the world. a little bit of crazy goes a long way, and happiness comes from within.
read
  • the drowned world, j. g. ballard [1962]: a thinky-sci-fi that, albeit in a less precise way, gets at the core philosophy of ballard's later books (specifically crash and high rise). it highlights how much of our psychology is based on the physical and societal structures around us, and questions what impact a new environment might have. its got a good ammount of overly-wanky jargon but the visuals kept me involved. particularly vivid were the descriptions of flora and fauna intertwined with the flooded carcases of abandoned buildings, oh and how fucking boiling hot it is. although the book attributes the rising water levels to solar flares, a modern reader can easily see how the current trend of climate change (due to human actions) will create similar situations. what was once a hypothetical apocalyptic scenario is now, essentially, inevitable.
  • happydale: devils in the desert [1999]: a tale about a misguided youth, set in a town full of overtly quirky characters. although everyone is weird, none of the characters actually stand out. the story itself is fairly mundane, which is made even more apparent by the odd backdrop of the town of happydale. the website seems to suggest that they wanted to do much more with this small suburban kaleidescope.
